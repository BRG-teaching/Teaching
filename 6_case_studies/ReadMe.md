Focus:
- BRG demonstrators and projects, the topics are chosen to address different (combination of) topics from the core modules but also cover different structural typologies. Students are tasked with applying their skillset to a larger and more intricate architectural project. The primary objective here is to gain a comprehensive understanding of the cross disciplinary technological aspects embedded in these new architectural projects.
- (3DCP) masonry system
- a cable-net formwork
- a timber assembly
- the three cases studies will be designed increasing in complexity, through combining more modules. So, the first case study focuses on the topics of the first two Foundation modules
- the second adds the topics of the third module
- the third case study integrates all parts covered
- practical insights through two, week-long building exercises in teams, introducing new key aspects such as discrepancies between digital model and reality, tolerances, etc.

Outcome:
- have advanced in your computational skills applied in a real project
- understand the challenges of full scale prototyping
- develop cross-disciplinary project management and coordination skills
- develop skills in group presentation and documentation of a complex digital project
- develop customized computation allow for agile design and analysis
